University Innovation Fellows (UIF)

UIF Home Page

The University Innovation Fellows (run through Stanford's world-renowned d.school) empowers students to become leaders of change in higher education. Fellows are creating a global movement to ensure that all students gain the necessary attitudes, skills and knowledge to compete in the economy of the future. For the past two years, a cohort of up to four St. Thomas students have undergone training, designed and implemented an innovation on campus, and traveled to the Silicon Valley Meetup to share their work with other University Innovation Fellows at Google, Microsoft and Stanford University faculty.

Personal UIF Wiki Page

Currently, there are five of us, UST students, involved in 2020 UIF Cohort. We are going through a rigorous 6-weeks training. During the training, we have to come up with prototypes to make impactful, prolonged changes at the University of St. Thomas. We have been using Human-Centered Design and the Lean Startup framework throughout the process. For example, we have to do interviews with our friends and ask them about their experience from last semester when classes were moved online. One of that statements we've based our solutions on is "I don't feel like a student anymore." From there, we've been interviewing more students, mostly freshman, on our prototype. We will then hold a meeting with our stakeholders, such us President Sullivan and some professors.

International Dinner

The 30th annual International Dinner is a spring tradition hosted by the Globally Minded Student Association. The purpose of this event is to celebrate internationalism and diversity at the University of St. Thomas. The event was held on Saturday, April 27, in the Woulfe Alumni Hall in the third floor of Anderson Student Center. The dinner was a semiformal event where guests were encouraged to wear their traditional clothing.

The fall semester of my first year, I was a member of the 30th International Dinner Committee. I compiled the archives of past International Dinners from the library because I thought it would be fun to be nostalgic for the 30th International Dinner. Forcing myself to get out of my comfort zone and to grow, I also offered myself to be the emcee. Part way through the semester, the committee leader announced she would go on a semester-long study abroad program in the spring semester, and therefore we needed a new leader soon. Having plenty experiences planning events in Indonesia, I braced myself to take over the leadership role as a first-year student. With our advisor’s help, I was able to learn how to build relationships with many stakeholders to best hold this event. With my current connections with international and domestic students, I was able to reconstruct a solid team of the 30th International Dinner committee. The 30th International Dinner was a success. The nostalgic feels were maintained with old pictures displayed both on the screens and tables. We had food representing most continents. Moreover, only having slots for 20 flag carriers didn’t stop us from engaging more students; a friend and I made and presented a video with international and domestic students saying welcome, enjoy, and see you in their languages throughout the evening. All audiences could be engaged by participating on a Kahoot! Quiz and an Indonesian traditional dance tutorial. We richly celebrated internationalism throughout this event.

Youtube Channel during Quarantine

During quarantine, I created tutorial videos and uploaded them on Youtube to help students in Indonesia who are looking for colleges and scholarships in the U.S.. One of my videos got over 9,000 views as of early October 2020. The project started because I wanted to help provide some insights on studying abroad with my spare time during quarantine. Getting featured by Voice of America (VOA) in Indonesia was also one of the reasons I started this project.
